Top Cities in Bulgaria

Sofia

With 1,291,591 permanent residents, it is the largest city in the country, as well as the 14th largest city in the European Union. Sofia is located in the central part of the Balkan Peninsula, in the Sofia valley, surrounded by several mountains, which is the largest and one of the highest sub-Balkan fields..

Sofia is located in the central part of the Balkan Peninsula, in the Sofia valley, surrounded by several mountains, which is the largest and one of the highest sub-Balkan fields. It occupies a central position in the Balkans, with several main roads connecting it with other parts of the peninsula – through the Vakarelli mountains to Thrace and Istanbul, through the Dragoman pass to Belgrade and Central Europe, along the Struma Valley to Greece and North Macedonia and through several passes in the Balkan Mountains to Northern Bulgaria and Romania. East of the town passes the Iskar River, and through the town itself several small tributaries of it.

The first written evidence of the city is dated to the century BC. HR. when it was the main city of one of the Thracian peoples. It became one of the main strongholds of the Roman Empire in the Balkans and during the last century served as a temporary residence for several emperors. The city retained its importance in the Middle Ages, and after the last century saw a new prosperity, as one of the main logistics bases in the rear of the Ottoman Empire in its military campaigns in central Europe. With the military weakening of the Empire from the end of the century, Sofia also declined and over the next two centuries its population declined and many buildings were neglected or abandoned. In 1879, shortly after the liberation of Bulgaria, the city became the capital of the new state and in the following decades grew rapidly.

Today Sofia is the main administrative, industrial, transport, cultural and educational center of the country, with 1/6 of the industrial production of Bulgaria. Here are also located the Bulgarian Academy of Sciences, the central buildings of the Bulgarian national television, The Bulgarian National Radio, The Bulgarian Telegraph Agency, many universities, theatres, cinemas, as well as the National Art Gallery, archaeological, historical, natural history and other museums. In many places in the city center are preserved visible archaeological monuments from Roman times

Plovdiv

Plovdiv is the second largest city in Bulgaria. The city has an area of 102 sq.km. According to the Grao as of 15 June 2024. 371,536 people live in the city at current address and 386,546 people at permanent address. This ranks it in second place after the capital Sofia. Plovdiv is located in the western part of the upper Thracian Valley, on both banks of the Maritsa River. It is 15 km north of the Rhodopes and 50 km south of the Balkan Mountains. The city is built at the foot of six sienite Hills, which is why it is often called "the city under the hills". Plovdiv is the management center of Plovdiv district, Plovdiv municipality, Maritsa municipality, Rodopi municipality and is the largest economic core of the South Central Region.

Plovdiv is a city of over 8,000 years, whose past can be traced from prehistory to the present day. The first Neolithic settlements within the boundaries of today's city began in the millennia BC.N.well., and from the stone-Copper Age (Urga – Urga millennium BC.N.well.) life in the city has not been interrupted, which ranks it among the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Preserved and preserved are ancient monuments such as the ancient theatre, the Roman Odeon, the Agora (Roman Forum), the Roman stadium, the late antique building "Eirene", the bishop's Basilica, the small Basilica and others.

The city is the most dynamically developing Center in southern Bulgaria. Its economy is supported by a well-developed multi-profile industry, services, tourism and Information Technology. Many cultural events are also held in Plovdiv: the International Fair, the international theatre festival "scene at the crossroads", "Kapana Fest", "The Night of Plovdiv", The Music Forum, the International Folklore Festival "Plovdiv" and others.

Plovdiv has been chosen as European capital of culture for 2019 together with another European city – Matera in Italy. In February 2022, Plovdiv took fourth place in the ranking of tourist site Apostille as the best European destination, ahead of Amiens, Marbella and Ljubljana, and the magazine of Apostille declared Plovdiv as the best destination for cultural tourism in Europe.

The motto of Plovdiv is "ancient and eternal".

Burgas

Burgas is the largest city in south-eastern Bulgaria and the second largest on the Bulgarian Black Sea coast, and with its area of 253,644 .2 it is also one of the largest in the country. According to the Grao as of 15 March 2025. in the city live 210 373 people at current address and 225 903 people at permanent address. It is the fourth most populous city in the country (after Sofia, Plovdiv and Varna). Burgas is the most important cultural, economic, Transport, Management, tourist and Educational Center in southeastern Bulgaria and is of national importance. The city is the administrative center of the municipality and District of the same name, as well as the seat of regional and national institutions.

Burgas is located:

  • the International and second busiest Bulgarian airport;
  • the second largest Bulgarian port; the largest oil port in the country.

The several nature reserves and protected areas, the ancient and medieval settlements, the Black Sea, Strandzha and the annual festivals attract many tourists. Ruse

Ruse is a town in northeastern Bulgaria, the administrative center of ruse district and Ruse municipality. The population of the city is about 121,168 people (31 December 2024), as it is the largest Bulgarian city along the Danube river and the sixth largest in the country.

Ruse is located on the north – eastern border of Bulgaria with Romania, 313 apostilles from the capital Sofia and 73 apostilles from the Romanian capital Bucharest, the city is a potential strategic intermodal and logistics center of the country and of southeastern Europe, given its geographical location, its established role as an important industrial center, as well as the presence of the country's first road and railway bridge over the Danube River-Danube Bridge. The river also connects the city to pan-European Transport Corridor 7, creating conditions for commercial and cruise river navigation.

Ruse is one of the cities in Bulgaria that attract both its magnificent architecture and its rich historical past. Thousands of years ago (from C. PR. N. well.) on the site of today's city existed the ancient Roman fortress Sexaginta Prista, which acted as a fortification and port along the border of the Roman state. There are archaeological finds that testify to the existence of a Thracian settlement before the emergence of Sexaginta Prista. After the destruction of the medieval town of Cherven, which was among the important centers of the second Bulgarian Kingdom, along with the capital Tarnovgrad, a large part of the inhabitants of the ruined fortress migrated to the growing settlement on the territory of today's ruse. During the Ottoman rule, the city continued to develop, becoming an important port Trade Center and military fortification.

Ruse is the hometown of a number of Bulgarian national revival figures, supporters of the Bulgarian spirit, revolutionaries and participants in the liberation. Along with this, however, it was also a cosmopolitan city for its time, tolerating different faiths and adopting technological and social innovations. After the liberation, the city was the most populous but also the most modern city in the Principality of Bulgaria, before the new capital surpassed it in population. Many of the technological innovations of the late and early ages found their first application in the beautiful Danube city.

Rousse is often called "Little Vienna" in Bulgaria because of the numerous buildings-monuments of culture, whose architecture includes the styles neoparok, modern, Neoclassicism and secession. The symbols of the City are the revenue building and the monument of freedom. Residents and guests of ruse have the opportunity to enjoy architecture unique to Bulgaria. The aristocratic and romantic spirit of the city attracts more and more tourists.

The motto of ruse is "City of free spirit".

Rousse is the birthplace of Nobel laureate for Literature Elias Canetti.

Veliko Tarnovo

Velingrad Tarnovo is a town in Northern Bulgaria, the administrative center of Veliko Tarnovo district and Veliko Tarnovo municipality. The city was the capital of the second Bulgarian state and the Principality of Bulgaria in the period 1878-1879. In the Middle Ages it was called Tarnovgrad, gradually becoming known by the name Tarnovo. By a decree of July 27, 1965, "Veliko" was added in front of it in honor of the glory of the city at the time when it was the capital of the second Bulgarian state. Thanks to its rich cultural and historical heritage, Veliko Tarnovo is an important tourist center.

The city is the 15th largest in Bulgaria with a population of 58,507 inhabitants according to census data in September 2021. Veliko Tarnovo is one of the four cities in Bulgaria with positive natural growth from 2006 to 2014. The agglomeration of Veliko Tarnovo, Gorna Oryahovitsa, Dolna Oryahovitsa, Lyaskovets and the villages Arbanasi, Parvomaytsi, Samovodene, Sheremetya has a population of 99,278 people.

Located between the Danube plain and the Balkan Mountains, on the first hills of the pre-Balkan Mountains, Veliko Tarnovo is a major administrative, industrial and educational center of the region. Here are located the National Military University "Vasil Levski" - faculty "General Military", University "SV. St. Cyril and Methodius“, the Cathedral of the Nativity of the Virgin, the fortresses Tsarevets and Trapezitsa, numerous museums and cultural landmarks: a total of 1613 objects of the historical heritage of Bulgaria. There are also several industrial zones in Veliko Tarnovo that are important for the city's economy.

In 1879. the Constituent Assembly was held in the city, marking the beginning of the third Bulgarian state. The guardian of the city is St. Patriarch Evtimiy, whose monument rises above the building of Veliko Tarnovo University. Veliko Tarnovo is a candidate city for European capital of Culture 2019.[5] a peak on the Antarctic island of Livingston is named Tarnovo in honor of the old capital of the Republic Of Bulgaria.

By decision of the 44th National Assembly of 16 April 2019 GR. Veliko Tarnovo is declared the historical and spiritual capital of Bulgaria.

Sozopol

Sozopol is a town in southeastern Bulgaria on the Black Sea. It is located on several small peninsulas in the southern part of the Burgas Bay. With a population of 6428 people according to NSI data as of 31 December 2019, the town is the eighth largest settlement in Burgas region and is the administrative center of Sozopol municipality. The distance to Burgas is 34 km. It is the successor of the Greek colony of Apollonia and together with Nessebar is one of the oldest Bulgarian cities. From antiquity to the last century, Sozopol has been a thriving commercial center. It is known as a fishing and Wine Center and occupies an important place in the trade with grain from Thrace. Since late antiquity, the city has developed as an important church Center with numerous medieval monasteries.

With its cultural and historical past, with its preserved architecture from the Urga century, its beaches and the annual arts festivals "Apollonia" Sozopol attracts many tourists today.

Top Places to Visit in Bulgaria

the Madara Horseman

The Madara Horseman represents a unique rock relief dating from the beginning of the 8th century. The relief is carved on a hundred-meter-high, almost vertical limestone rock and is the only such relief in the whole of Europe without a parallel in the European cultural heritage.

The carefully crafted composition of the relief represents a hunting scene, in which in natural size is depicted a horseman on the right, who impales with a spear the fallen lion in the front legs of the horse. In front of the rider is a flying eagle in a heraldic pose, and behind him - an image of a dog. Around this scene there are inscriptions in Greek telling about important moments in the history of the Bulgarian state. The first inscription is from the time of Khan Tervel (701-717) and tells of the help the Khan provided to Emperor Justinian to regain the throne in Constantinople. The second inscription is located on the left under the horseman and is from the time of Khan Kormisosh (CA. 756). It refers to the Bulgarian-Byzantine relations and the tax that Byzantium paid to Bulgaria at that time. The third inscription, located at the bottom right, is from the time of Khan Omurtag (814-831) and again refers to the relations between Bulgaria and Byzantium. These inscriptions are the first, earliest domestic written sources for Bulgarian history.

There are those that the horseman depicted in the Madara bas-relief is probably one of the Bulgarian rulers - Asparuh, Tervel or Krum. Although the debate on the issue is not over, it has been suggested that the horseman represents a Bulgarian ruler and the pierced and defeated lion symbolizes Byzantium. In 1979, the Madara Horseman was included in the list of world cultural heritage of the Apostille.

The devil's bridge

Devil's bridge is a bridge on the Arda River in Ardino municipality, Kardzhali province.

It is located in a picturesque gorge about 10 km northwest of the town of Ardino, near the village of Dyadovtsi and not far from the village of Latinka.

The bridge is located at 420 meters above sea level in a gorge surrounded on both sides by steep slopes reaching up to 800 meters above sea level. Its length is 56 meters, width-3.5 meters, three-vaulted, and on the arches of its side ribs are made openings with semicircular arches for water drainage. The height of the central arch is 11 – 12 meters, and along the edge is preserved a stone parapet with a height of 12 cm.

The bridge was built at the beginning of the Apostille century on the order of Sultan Selim Apostille as part of a road connecting the upper Thracian lowlands with White Sea Thrace and the Aegean Sea. On the site of today's bridge there was once a Roman bridge, part of an ancient road connecting the White Sea and Northern Thrace through the Makaza pass. By order of Sultan Selim, the bridge was rebuilt in order to continue trade links between the two geographical areas. He was known by the name Shaitan kupriya.

For centuries, the bridge has been shrouded in Legends and mysteries. Almost nothing is known about its construction, but according to tradition its creator was Master Dimitar from the nearby village of Nedelino. He accepted the challenge of building a bridge over the turbulent river, even though everyone before him had failed in the attempt. He did great and in record time, but died soon after. It is believed that Satan had a hand in the construction of the bridge. The devil promised the master to share a secret that would make his creation eternal if he could complete it in 40 days. If he didn't, he would take his soul. Dimitar did the devil's task and Satan fulfilled his promise, but soon after this The Gifted Master died and took the secret knowledge to the grave.

They say that from The Devil's bridge you can see the face of Satan if you look at the waters of the Arda River between 11.00 and 12.00 at noon, when the bridge and its reflection form a circle. In addition, according to some, one of its Stones shows the imprint of The Devil's foot.

On 24 February 1984. the bridge has been declared a monument of culture.[1] near the bridge is built tourist infrastructure for recreation and picnic. After recovery in 2013. the road from Ardino to The Devil's bridge is paved.

The palace of the Romanian Queen Maria in Balchi

The Palace in Balchik, also called "The Quiet Nest", is the former summer residence of the Romanian Queen Maria of Edinburgh. It was built between 1926 and 1937. in a style combining Italian and oriental elements, including villas for the royal family and staff, a chapel, gardens, vineyards and more. The palace is known for its unique architecture and beautiful gardens, including the "Allah" garden, which is home to a large collection of cacti.

The architectural and Park complex "The Palace" is located about 2 km southwest of the center of Balchik, Dobrich region.

Представлява комплекс вили и други постройки, разположени на брега на Черно море. След възвръщането на Южна Добруджа през 1940 дворецът попада в границите на България и е разположен на площ от 194 дка. Комплексът се превръща в една от главните забележителности на Северното Черноморие и е включен в Стоте национални туристически обекта.

During the Inter-Allied War, Romania violated the St. Petersburg protocol, signed only a few months earlier, and attacked Bulgaria from behind. June 28, 1913. the dobrudzha army took advantage of the absence of any Bulgarian troops against it and undisturbed occupied the Bulgarian state territories northeast of the Tutrakan-Balchik line. The Queen of Romania, Maria of Edinburgh, quickly liked the newly conquered land with its mild climate, warm sea water and picturesque balchik rocks. However, the reasons and the choice of place for the construction of the new Apostille residence are predetermined as political – marking the occupied territory as Romanian and at the southernmost point of the Apostille possible.

At the beginning of the 20th century, the area southwest of the town. Balchik is known under the name "the three watermills" and Kulak State Forest. In the Romanian registers after the occupation were recorded documents for ownership of people with Bulgarian names, issued by the Ministry of Agriculture and state property. There are certificates of "free transfer of Lands", other documents dated to 1935. they certify that under the then-current law on agricultural reform in the Romanian Kingdom, the same year the Queen received documents for ownership of the land and buildings and it is added that "the price of the property and the corresponding fees for its sale have been paid in full". The area owned by the monarchy was gradually increased. By 1937. the holdings already extend over 209.8 decares, and by 1938 their area was still increasing and reached 244.8 decares.

The villa ensemble in the summer residence of the Romanian Queen was designed by Italian architects Amerigo and Augustino, and the construction was carried out from 1924 to 1934. During the years of occupation in Balchik, many Romanian aristocrats and intellectuals bought places, including the famous artist Cicilia Koutsescu-Stork. They were often invited to the royal residence, making the Palace a cultural center. September 7, 1940. with international support, Tsar Boris was able to conclude the Craiova Agreement and return balchik and the whole of Southern dobrudzha to Bulgaria. In 1948. with an interstate agreement between Romania and Bulgaria, the site is transferred to the Bulgarian side. By order 1650/30 August 1961 The Council of Ministers of the Republic of Bulgaria approves the purchase of real estate owned by Romania, located on the territory of the people's Republic Of Bulgaria, among which is the former palace in Balchik. In 1970. an agreement was signed between the people's Republic Of Bulgaria and the Socialist Republic of Romania for the transfer of ownership of some real estate, among which the whole land and all buildings of the former palace in Balchik. Today the palace is well preserved and constantly restored, and after the 80s it was turned into a remarkable tourist center. On the part of various nationalist intellectuals and public figures, concerns were expressed that the Palace thus became a propaganda showcase for the Romanian occupation of Southern Dobrudzha, imposing the impression that Dobrudzha was an "indigenous Romanian" land, thus justifying territorial claims against Bulgaria.

The wonderful bridges

The wonderful bridges, The Rock bridges (old. Erkyupria) are a rock phenomenon located in the karst Valley of the Erkyupria River in the Western Rhodopes at 1450 altitude, at the foot of Golyam Persenk peak.

The bridges were formed by the erosive activity of a deep river in the past, which transformed the cracks in the marble into a deep water Cave, whose ceiling over time thinned in places and collapsed. It is believed that the debris from the collapse was subsequently carried away by the waters of the river.

As a result, two famous marble bridges remain. The big one is about 15 evergreens wide in the wider parts, long almost 100 Bangladeshis. It consists of three arches, the largest of which is 45 aposematic in height and 40 aposematic in width. The small bridge is 200 ambiguities away from the big one along the river - impassable, 60 ambiguities long, with a total height of 50 ambiguities, and only on the arch – 30 ambiguities. After it there is a very small third bridge, which is a ponorna cave, in which the waters of Ercupria disappear, only to reappear on the surface after 3 apostilles.

The area around the two bridges is occupied by centuries-old coniferous forests, consisting mainly of spruce trees. Both bridges are secured and adapted for tourist purposes. Passing is allowed on and below them.

In the Register of protected areas and protected areas in Bulgaria, the wonderful bridges – air KYUPRIA is a protected area of category: Natural Landmark.

There is a barbaric custom that arose after the bridges were declared a tourist site – every tourist who passed under the Great Bridge wrote his name with charcoal on the wall. On the rock formations at the base of the Great Bridge have left a trace of their low culture even celebrities. According to the law, scrawling and placing inscriptions within the boundaries of a natural landmark is prohibited. The fine for this violation is from 500 to 5000 Leva. Nature lovers clean the inscriptions, fines are imposed, but this vandal tradition continues.

Near the rock phenomenon there are many caves, but most of them are not suitable for visits by tourists, as they are not lit and protected. Nearby are the chalets "Wonder bridges" and "rock bridges", managed by the tourist company "Studenets" – town. Chepelare (possible places for tourism and overnight accommodation).

Raysko praskalo waterfall

Paradise praskalo is a waterfall in Bulgaria with a height of 124.5 m.

It is located in the Balkan Mountains, on the southern slope below the highest Stara Planina peak-Botev. On the territory of the nature reserve "Djendema", part of the National Park "Central Balkan". It was declared a protected natural site in 1965.

It is formed by the waters of the Praskalska river, coming from the eternal snezhnik, located under Botev peak, which even in the summer does not melt completely. After flowing along the gentle grassy slope of the summit, the stream reaches the upper edge of a rocky belt of sheer cliffs called the Paradise rocks and makes its great leap. It then descends into the abysses of the Southern Djendem and flows into the White River.

Next to the foot of the waterfall is the hut "Rai" – a starting point for his visit. The terrain and nature are impressive, and the rocks attract many rock climbing enthusiasts. And despite the fact that it is visited by numerous tourists, the waterfall and the area have preserved its intact and clean appearance.

Rai praskalo is sometimes incorrectly called Kalofersko praskalo or Big Dzhendem waterfall, but these are the names of another big waterfall (120), located in the South Dzhendem, on the Dzhendem River, which, however, is very difficult to reach.

Krastova Gora

Krastova Gora is a locality in the Rhodopes, known for its spiritual and mystical atmosphere. It is believed that a fragment of Christ's Cross is buried there, which attracts many pilgrims. The place is also known for the monastery complex "Holy Trinity" and the chapels dedicated to the 12 Apostles.

Local people tell about Yordan Dryankov, a native of the village of Kovachevitsa, Gotsedelchevsko. Early renounced worldly benefits and worldly goods, Jordancho, as he was once called, dedicated himself to the Lord. He often received visions, one of which after a night spent on Mount Krastov. In this particular vision, the history of the place is revealed to him.

On this peak there was a large monastery, in which a piece of the Cross of the Lord was kept. Previously, this particle, which was attached to a cross or made from it a cross, was kept in Constantinople in the Sultan's Treasury. The Tsar of Russia sent envoys with rich gifts for the Sultan, telling them: "when he asks the Sultan to give you gifts for me, you tell him that your Tsar wants nothing but the wooden cross, which he has learned is kept in your treasury.“ The envoys did the King's bidding, and the sultan gave them the requested cross. When the Sultan's mother heard of this, she said to him, "what have you done? Do you know that this cross held your power and your power? Let the Potters catch the messengers and take it back.“

The sultan sent men, but they could not catch the envoys, because when they learned that they were looking for them, they did not return by the same road, but went to the Middle Rhodopes. In the Monastery of Krastov peak, the Russians handed over the cross to the monks and left for Russia. Soon after, the monastery was attacked by the Turks and destroyed to the ground, and the monks were killed. However, they managed to hide the cross in the underground monastery hideout.

In Jordancho's vision, he was also told to make and place a high metal cross on the hill, as a sign that there was once buried a piece of Christ's cross. So he tells his vision to King Boris. At the same time with the Tsar was Lieutenant Colonel Velichkov from the reserve, who said that God had shown him exactly what the metal cross should look like. They decided to cast a 66-kilogram metal cross (double the age of Christ), with the costs divided between the king and the lieutenant colonel.

May 1, 1936. the metal cross is placed on top of the cross. After the ceremony of his consecration, a white dove landed on the bowl of holy water, which then flew to the western elevation of Krastov peak. Yordan Stoychev told the people to follow the pigeon because he would tell them where the old monastery spring was. After clearing the rock on which the dove landed, water gushed from it. A second metal cross was erected above it, as well as a small chapel of donors. The object is known as Ayazmoto. It is located 320 meters west of the center of the complex.

In 1994. the Cross, given by King Boris, disappeared and after 7 months was found in the forest. It is placed in the newly built temple, and in place of the old one is cast a new stainless steel with a weight of 99 kilograms (triple Christ's age).

What is known about the further fate of Yordan Stoychev is that he disappeared and in the 60s martyred in the Lovech camp, buried alive up to his head in dirt. His life is not fully explored and there are many mysteries and uncertainties around him.

From a historical point of view, the vision of Yordancho can in its main part be considered reliable for the following reasons:

  • – The existence of a fragment of the Cross of Christ in Constantinople is evidenced by the lives of the Saints and the church tradition. It is quite possible that after the fall of the city under Turkish rule, the treasures of the Byzantine court, together with the fragment of the cross, fell into the hands of the Sultan.
  • – Until 1677. there is a marked tendency among the Russian tsars to send envoys to the Sultan's Court in Istanbul in order to win his friendship. It is not excluded that during one such visit the particle of the wooden cross was also requested.
  • – In most cases, the Sultans ' wives were Christian or former. So it is possible that the Sultan's mother was connected to the Christian faith and knew about the power of the miraculous cross.
  • – Archaeological finds prove the existence of Krastov peak of a large monastery complex.
